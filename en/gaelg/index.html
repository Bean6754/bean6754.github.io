To be                               - Dy ve.
I am                                - Ta mee.
You are                             - T'ou (ta oo).
He/She/It is                        - T'eh (ta eh)/T'ee (ta ee)/T'eh (ta eh).
We/You (pl.)/They are               - Ta shin (ta'in)/T'ad (ta ad).

To be called                        - Ennym.
I am called                         - Ta'n ennym orrym .
You are called                      - Ta'n ennym ort .
He/She/It is called                 - .
We/You (pl.)/They are called        - Ta'n ennym erriu .
(Also used for '.. name is')

To have                             - Aym.
I have a fish                       - Ta eeast aym.
You have a fish                     - Ta eeast ayd/Vel eeast ayd?
He/She/It has a fish                - Ta eeast echey/Ta eeast echey/Ta eeast echey.
We/You (pl.)/They have a fish       - 
('fisk' is masculine so 'en' is used)
