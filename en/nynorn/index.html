Common Conjugations

https://nornlanguage.x10.mx/index.php?tut_shet/lesson_shet_05    

To be                               - At vara.
I am                                - Eg er.
You are                             - Du ert.
He/She/It is                        - Hann/Hun/Dað er.
We/You (pl.)/They are               - Vi/Di/Dir eru.

To be called                        - At heda.
I am called                         - Eg hedi.
You are called                      - Du heder.
He/She/It is called                 - Hann/Hun/Dað heder.
We/You (pl.)/They are called        - Vi/Di/Dir heda.
(Also used for '.. name is')

To have                             - At hava.
I have a fish                       - Eg hev en fisk.
You have a fish                     - Du hever en fisk.
He/She/It has a fish                - Hann/Hun/Dað hever en fisk.
We/You (pl.)/They have a fish       - Vi/Di/Dir hava en fisk.
('fisk' is masculine so 'en' is used)

To love                             - At elska.
I love cows                         - Eg elska kør.
You love cows                       - Du elskar kør.
He/She/It loves cows                - Hann/Hun/Dað elskar kør.
We/You (pl.)/They love cows         - Vi/Di/Dir elska kør.
('kør' is the plural of 'ku' and 'kør' is an irregular plural)

To call                             - At kalla søð.
I call sheep                        - Eg kalla søð.
You call sheep                      - Du kallar søð.
He/She/It calls sheep               - Hann/Hun/Dað kallar søð.
We/You (pl.)/They call sheep        - Vi/Di/Dir kalla søð.
('søð' is the plural for sheep in Nynorn, although there is 'sau (sg.)' and 'sauer (pl.)' in Norwegian)

To make                             - At gera.
I make a house                      - Eg geri et hus.
You make a house                    - Du gerer et hus.
He/She/It makes a house             - Hann/Hun/Dað gerer et hus.
We/You (pl.)/They make a house      - Vi/Di/Dir gera et hus.
